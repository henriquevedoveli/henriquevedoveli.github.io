Classification of Pneumonia on Chest X-ray Using Convolutional Neural Networks (pt-br)
Bachelor's Thesis

Pneumonia affects a large percentage of the world's population, and one of the diagnostic methods for the disease is through chest radiography. To automate the diagnostic process, aiming to reduce the time to obtain the test results and decrease the chances of human errors, a method for automatic classification of chest radiography images using convolutional neural networks is proposed. The choice of the classification model was based on the evaluation of performance from well-known neural network architectures. These architectures include LeNet-5 (78.80% accuracy for multi-class classification and 86.85% for binary classification), AlexNet (87.01% accuracy for multi-class classification and 92.31% for binary classification), VGG-16 (87.02% accuracy for multi-class classification and 95.83% for binary classification), Inception V3 (84.46% accuracy for multi-class classification and 90.87% for binary classification), and ResNet-50 (84.14% accuracy for multi-class classification and 85.58% for binary classification). Using these neural networks, a new convolutional neural network named VGG-Inc was proposed, combining features from VGG-16 and Inception V3 architectures. VGG-Inc achieved an accuracy rate of 91.35% for multi-class classification. Additionally, it obtained an F1-Score of 0.947 for the healthy lungs class, 0.929 for bacterial pneumonia class, and 0.837 for viral pneumonia class. For binary classification, the model achieved an accuracy rate of 96.15% on the test data. Consequently, together with the responsible physician, it is hoped that the proposed new classification model capable of identifying the presence of pneumonia and differentiating between viral and bacterial pneumonia in the classified images will significantly reduce the time for medical decision-making and also decrease the chances of human error during diagnosis.

Multi-class Classification Model for Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) Images Using Deep Learning (pt-br)
Scientific Research

Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) is a procedure performed for the detection and monitoring of diseases in the retina. By using the images generated during the examination, the doctor can detect diseases such as Diabetic Macular Edema (DME), Choroidal Neovascularization (CNV), Optic Disc Drusen (ODD), and also a healthy retina without any disease. This study aims to find an alternative for automating the diagnostic process of OCT examinations through the construction and training of a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) capable of classifying three retinal diseases and a healthy retina, using the CNN architectures AlexNet and Inception as a basis. The CNN used contains a total of 8,069,000 trainable parameters and a depth of 16 layers. The training and validation of the network were performed using data from an open dataset containing thousands of valid and labeled OCT examination images. The model was trained for 40 epochs, achieving an accuracy rate of 95.15% in the blind test. For each class, the F1-score obtained was 92.5% for CNV, 97.1% for DME, 93.6% for ODD, and 97.3% for the normal retina. When the model was subjected to a classification rate of 95%, the obtained accuracy rate was 97.45%.
